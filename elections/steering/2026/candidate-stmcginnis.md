## What I have done

I've contributed to Kubernetes since 2017, with an involvement I'd
characterize as breadth over depth. I'm now a maintainer across SIG Network,
SIG Testing, SIG Contributor Experience, SIG Release (signalhound), and SIG
Cloud Provider (the Lambda provider), the latter in support of GPU workload
testing with SIG Testing and K8s Infra. I serve on the Kubernetes Code of
Conduct Committee, I've helped organize the Kubernetes-focused activities at
the Contributor and Maintainer Summits and am now a co-chair for the event. I
regularly contribute PRs across various other repos where I see an opportunity
to add value. I've spoken at several KubeCons and Maintainer Summits, and
served on the program committee or as a track chair for several more.

## What I'll do

I see Steering's role as caring for the community's processes, policies, and
health, as well as supporting its technical direction. My focus would be
developer and contributor experience: the friction a person hits on their first
PR, their first review, or their first attempt to move from casual contributor
to maintainer shapes whether they stay. Having been that casual contributor for
years, I want to make sure we are supporting policies and other efforts to make
those paths smoother and the on-ramps clearer. I think it's critical to foster
a community that will encourage involvement for the long-term health of the
project - and its maintainers. I'll also draw on lessons from my time in
other open source communities as a project lead for multiple projects, a
steering member, and board director - for things worth trying, and worth
avoiding.
